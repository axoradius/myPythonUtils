"Muscle Cars" is a bullshit category Anonymous 04/21/23(Fri)18:08:53 No.26558943   [Reply]
I have never found a consistent definition of what a Muscle Car is. It's nothing more than a term used by boomers and eventually corporations to increase the value of their car through the mythologization of specific cars into working-man's supercars when they were simply nothing of the sort, and the consistent use of the term serves no utility other than completely arbitrarily increasing the price of certain cars.

The usual definition that people give is "a mid-sized lightweight, cheap car with a big engine that produces lots of horsepower," but there are cars that everyone "agrees" are muscle cars that don't fit this definition at all.

>mid-sized
Pony cars are lumped in with the Muscle Car bracket constantly. People say that Camaros and Mustangs and Barracudas are muscle cars when they're actually ponies. On the other end of the bracket, people include the Oldsmobile Tornado and Buick Riviera, full-sized cars, as muscle cars as well. It's worth noting that the Tornado also flouts the idea that muscle cars are supposed to be RWD.

>lightweight
Information on car weights vary drastically, but many estimations place 70's Challengers at just under or slightly over 4000lbs depending on the year and the options. That's FAR from a light car. Modern Challengers are constantly over 4000lbs, and no one is going to argue that the Challenger isn't a muscle car.
